Team SILKRepo review · architecture · stack
The problem

The apprenticeship is thinning.

Juniors used to learn by doing the grunt work — drafting, marking up, arguing small points. As AI absorbs that work, the reps that built judgement disappear.

The gap

You can't learn to negotiate by reading.

  • No safe place to argue out loud and lose
  • No opponent that holds its ground instead of agreeing
  • No immediate, grounded feedback on what you missed
Team SILK02
The thesis

Deliberate practice against genuine resistance.

01

Negotiate out loud

Real-time spoken argument against an AI opposing counsel that doesn't fold.

02

Stay coherent

An explicit game-state layer tracks positions & concessions — it never forgets what was agreed three turns ago.

03

Get coached

At round end the AI flips to coach — scores you against a model playbook, names the turning point, offers a rematch.

A well-defined task · immediate feedback · unlimited reps.

Team SILK03
The demo scenario

Marlin Health Trust v Nemo

A Data Processing Agreement for running children's health records through an AI diagnostics company. You act for Marlin — the data controller — and defend the GDPR clauses.

GDPR Art 28(3)Art 9(2) Breach notification
The trap

A baited "Warranties" clause

One clause is deliberately weak. The adversary asks "why did you pick this?" — rewarding juniors who spot it, exposing those who don't. Gold-standard answers are graded behind the scenes and never shown during the fight.

Team SILK08 · app/api/llm/v1/chat/completions/route.ts
Inside the shim

An OpenAI-compatible translation layer.

The shim speaks OpenAI on the wire and Anthropic underneath — so any Custom-LLM voice platform plugs straight in.

  • Format translationlib/openaiCompat.ts maps OpenAI chat ⇄ Claude messages, enforces Anthropic's "must start with a user turn" rule.
  • Barge-in & disconnect safehandles the client cutting in or dropping mid-stream without orphaning the Claude call.
  • Effectively statelessElevenLabs resends full history each turn, so lib/gameState.ts only counts turns — a documented swap point for a Neo4j argument graph.
  • Key stays server-side/api/elevenlabs/signed-url mints the WebSocket URL so the ElevenLabs key never reaches the browser.

Team SILK10 · lib/llm.ts
Grounded by a firm rulebook

The AI argues from the firm's own playbook.

lib/skill.ts loads the firm's markdown — method, DP checklist, gold-standard playbook — once at boot. It's the source of truth for how the AI questions and grades.

Drop any .md into DEMO DATA/ and it's auto-appended to the rulebook.
